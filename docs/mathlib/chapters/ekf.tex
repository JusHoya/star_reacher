\chapter{Reference Error-State EKF and Consistency Gates}
\label{ch:ekf}

\section{Purpose}
\label{sec:ekf:purpose}

This chapter specifies the built-in reference navigation filter (FR-25):
an error-state (indirect) extended Kalman filter, multiplicative in
attitude, that mechanizes a nominal state from the
Chapter~\ref{ch:sensors-imu} IMU increments and corrects it with
external-nav-fix, star-tracker, and altimeter updates
(Chapters~\ref{ch:sensors-radio} and~\ref{ch:sensors-optical}). It also
defines the estimator acceptance instrumentation of FR-26: the logged
estimate and innovation channels, the NEES and NIS statistics, and the
two-sided 95\,\% chi-square bounds that \texttt{star consistency}
computes per run and over ensembles --- the acceptance instrument for
\emph{every} estimator this project will ever host, classical or
learned. Phase~6 exit criterion~3 gates the reference filter through
ensemble NEES and NIS over a 100-run seeded ensemble. The filter is a
\emph{reference}: the simplest standard formulation, exactly specified,
chosen so an independent reimplementation from this chapter alone
reproduces it.

\section{Assumptions and domain of validity}
\label{sec:ekf:domain}

Assumptions:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Fifteen error states.} Attitude (3), velocity (3),
    position (3), gyro bias (3), accelerometer bias (3), in that order.
    This is the full observable set for the configured sensor suite:
    the star tracker observes attitude directly, the nav fix observes
    position and velocity, biases are observed through their integrated
    signatures. Scale-factor and misalignment states are deliberately
    excluded from the reference filter (they are configuration
    constants of the truth model, not estimated).
  \item \textbf{Filter dynamics model.} The navigator's internal
    gravity model is the central-body point mass,
    $\hat{\vv{g}}(\vv{p}) = -\mu\,\vv{p}/\norm{\vv{p}}^3$. The
    \emph{reference consistency scenario} configures the truth
    environment to the same class (point-mass gravity, no third bodies,
    no SRP), so process-model mismatch is zero and the NEES/NIS gates
    test the estimator implementation, not a model-error budget. Runs
    against richer truth environments require process-noise inflation
    and are diagnostics, not gates --- stated here so nobody tunes
    $\mat{Q}$ to force a gate.
  \item \textbf{Bias model match.} The filter models both bias errors
    as first-order Gauss--Markov with the \emph{configured sensor}
    parameters $(\sigma_{\mathrm{GM}}, \tau_c)$
    (equation~\eqref{eq:imu:gm}) --- the reference-implementation
    stance: the truth model is known. The reference scenario disables
    the turn-on biases ($\sigma_{g0} = \sigma_{a0} = 0$) and the
    altimeter bias ($\sigma_b = 0$), making the filter's stochastic
    model exact; enabled turn-on biases add a non-decaying component
    the Gauss--Markov model absorbs only over $t \ll \tau_c$, and such
    scenarios widen NEES beyond the gate by design.
  \item \textbf{Small-error linearization.} Standard EKF validity:
    attitude errors $\ll 1$ rad, position/velocity errors small
    against the linearization scale of
    $\hat{\vv{g}}$. Divergence under gross initialization error is
    out of scope for the reference filter.
  \item \textbf{First-order discretization.} Transition and
    process-noise matrices are the first-order forms of
    equation~\eqref{eq:ekf:disc}; adequacy requires
    $\Delta t\,\norm{\vv{\omega}} \ll 1$,
    $\Delta t / \tau_c \ll 1$, and $\Delta t$ small against the
    orbital dynamics ($n\,\Delta t \ll 1$, $n$ the mean motion) ---
    all satisfied by orders of magnitude at the D-5 cycle rates
    ($\qty{1}{}$--$\qty{100}{\hertz}$). The Van Loan construction
    remains the documented upgrade path if a future regime breaks
    these conditions~\cite{vanloan1978}.
\end{enumerate}

Domain of validity: cycle-synchronous operation on the D-5 schedule
with the Chapter~\ref{ch:sensors-imu} increment interface; aiding
sensors may drop out arbitrarily (validity flags); the filter never
throws inside the loop --- a non-finite or non-positive-definite input
covariance is a configuration error caught by the FR-15 validator, and
the Joseph-form update of equation~\eqref{eq:ekf:joseph} preserves
symmetry and positive semi-definiteness against accumulation of
rounding.

\section{Derivation}
\label{sec:ekf:derivation}

\subsection{State, error definition, and mechanization}
\label{sec:ekf:mechanization}

The nominal (total) state is
$\hat{\vv{x}} = (\hat{\quat{q}}_{i2b},\, \hat{\vv{v}}^{I},\,
\hat{\vv{p}}^{I},\, \hat{\vv{b}}_g,\, \hat{\vv{b}}_a)$; the error state
is
\begin{equation}
  \delta\vv{x} = \bigl[\delta\vv{\theta};\; \delta\vv{v};\;
    \delta\vv{p};\; \delta\vv{b}_g;\; \delta\vv{b}_a\bigr]
    \in \mathbb{R}^{15},
  \label{eq:ekf:staterr}
\end{equation}
with additive errors $\delta\vv{v} = \vv{v} - \hat{\vv{v}}$ etc., and
the multiplicative body-side attitude error
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \quat{q}_{i2b} = \hat{\quat{q}}_{i2b} \quatmul
    \delta\quat{q}(\delta\vv{\theta}),
  \;}
  \qquad
  \delta\quat{q}(\cdot) \text{ the exact map of
    equation~\eqref{eq:gnc:exactrot}},
  \label{eq:ekf:qerr}
\end{equation}
so $\delta\vv{\theta}$ is the small rotation from the estimated to the
true body frame, resolved in the body frame --- the same convention as
the sensor noise of Chapter~\ref{ch:sensors-optical} and the local
error of Sol\`a~\cite{sola2017}. To first order,
$\dcm{I}{B}(\quat{q}) = (\mat{I}_3 -
\skewm{\delta\vv{\theta}})\,\dcm{I}{B}(\hat{\quat{q}})$ by
equations~\eqref{eq:notation:quat2dcm}
and~\eqref{eq:notation:quatcomp}.

Per major cycle $\Delta t$, with measured increments
$\Delta\tilde{\vv{\theta}}_k$, $\Delta\tilde{\vv{v}}_k$
(Chapter~\ref{ch:sensors-imu}) and $\hat{\mat{C}} \equiv
{\dcm{I}{B}(\hat{\quat{q}}_{k-1})}^{\mathsf T}$ (body to inertial), the
nominal state mechanizes as
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \begin{aligned}
    \Delta\hat{\vv{\theta}}_k &= \Delta\tilde{\vv{\theta}}_k
      - \hat{\vv{b}}_{g,k-1}\,\Delta t,
    \qquad
    \Delta\hat{\vv{v}}_k = \Delta\tilde{\vv{v}}_k
      - \hat{\vv{b}}_{a,k-1}\,\Delta t,\\
    \hat{\quat{q}}_{k} &= \frac{\hat{\quat{q}}_{k-1} \quatmul
      \delta\quat{q}(\Delta\hat{\vv{\theta}}_k)}
      {\norm{\hat{\quat{q}}_{k-1} \quatmul
      \delta\quat{q}(\Delta\hat{\vv{\theta}}_k)}},\\
    \hat{\vv{v}}_{k} &= \hat{\vv{v}}_{k-1}
      + \hat{\mat{C}}\Bigl(\Delta\hat{\vv{v}}_k
        + \tfrac{1}{2}\,\Delta\hat{\vv{\theta}}_k \times
          \Delta\hat{\vv{v}}_k\Bigr)
      + \hat{\vv{g}}(\hat{\vv{p}}_{k-1})\,\Delta t,\\
    \hat{\vv{p}}_{k} &= \hat{\vv{p}}_{k-1}
      + \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl(\hat{\vv{v}}_{k-1}
        + \hat{\vv{v}}_{k}\bigr)\,\Delta t,\\
    \hat{\vv{b}}_{g,k} &= e^{-\Delta t/\tau_{c,g}}\,
      \hat{\vv{b}}_{g,k-1},
    \qquad
    \hat{\vv{b}}_{a,k} = e^{-\Delta t/\tau_{c,a}}\,
      \hat{\vv{b}}_{a,k-1},
  \end{aligned}
  \;}
  \label{eq:ekf:mech}
\end{equation}
the standard first-order strapdown cycle (Titterton and
Weston~\cite{titterton2004}; Savage~\cite{savage1998b}): the
$\tfrac{1}{2}\Delta\vv{\theta} \times \Delta\vv{v}$ term is the
rotation-of-frame (velocity rotation) compensation, leaving a residual
$O(\norm{\Delta\vv{\theta}}^2 \norm{\Delta\vv{v}})$ per cycle;
trapezoidal position integration matches; the bias estimates decay with
their Gauss--Markov model (a $\ll 1\,\%$ effect per cycle at the
supported $\tau_c$). The attitude increment shares
equation~\eqref{eq:gnc:exactrot} with the dead reckoner --- one code
path.

\subsection{Linearized error dynamics}
\label{sec:ekf:errordynamics}

Write the rate-level sensor models of Chapter~\ref{ch:sensors-imu} as
$\tilde{\vv{\omega}} = \vv{\omega} + \vv{b}_g + \vv{n}_g$ and
$\tilde{\vv{f}} = \vv{f} + \vv{b}_a + \vv{n}_a$, so the corrected
estimates satisfy $\hat{\vv{\omega}} = \vv{\omega} + \delta\vv{b}_g +
\vv{n}_g$ and $\hat{\vv{f}} = \vv{f} + \delta\vv{b}_a + \vv{n}_a$.
Differentiating the error definition~\eqref{eq:ekf:qerr} with the true
and nominal kinematics $\dot{\quat{q}} = \tfrac12 \quat{q} \quatmul
[0, \vv{\omega}]$, $\dot{\hat{\quat{q}}} = \tfrac12 \hat{\quat{q}}
\quatmul [0, \hat{\vv{\omega}}]$ gives $\dot{\delta\quat{q}} =
\tfrac12\bigl(\delta\quat{q} \quatmul [0, \vv{\omega}] -
[0, \hat{\vv{\omega}}] \quatmul \delta\quat{q}\bigr)$; expanding the
Hamilton products~\eqref{eq:notation:hamiltonproduct} at
$\delta\quat{q} = [1, \delta\vv{\theta}/2]$ and keeping first order,
the vector part yields the attitude error rate, and the velocity error
follows from $\dot{\vv{v}} = \dcm{B}{I}\vv{f} + \vv{g}(\vv{p})$ with
$\dcm{B}{I} = \hat{\mat{C}}(\mat{I}_3 + \skewm{\delta\vv{\theta}})$:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \begin{aligned}
    \dot{\delta\vv{\theta}} &= -\hat{\vv{\omega}} \times
      \delta\vv{\theta} - \delta\vv{b}_g - \vv{n}_g,\\
    \dot{\delta\vv{v}} &= -\hat{\mat{C}}\,\skewm{\hat{\vv{f}}}\,
      \delta\vv{\theta} + \mat{G}(\hat{\vv{p}})\,\delta\vv{p}
      - \hat{\mat{C}}\,\delta\vv{b}_a - \hat{\mat{C}}\,\vv{n}_a,\\
    \dot{\delta\vv{p}} &= \delta\vv{v},\\
    \dot{\delta\vv{b}}_g &= -\delta\vv{b}_g/\tau_{c,g} + \vv{w}_g,
    \qquad
    \dot{\delta\vv{b}}_a = -\delta\vv{b}_a/\tau_{c,a} + \vv{w}_a,
  \end{aligned}
  \;}
  \label{eq:ekf:errdyn}
\end{equation}
the local-error form of Sol\`a~\cite{sola2017} transcribed to the
project convention, with the point-mass gravity gradient
\begin{equation}
  \mat{G}(\vv{p}) = \frac{\mu}{\norm{\vv{p}}^3}
    \bigl(3\,\hat{\vv{p}}\hat{\vv{p}}^{\mathsf T} - \mat{I}_3\bigr),
  \qquad
  \hat{\vv{p}} = \vv{p}/\norm{\vv{p}} .
  \label{eq:ekf:gravgrad}
\end{equation}
In block form over the ordering~\eqref{eq:ekf:staterr}, with
$\hat{\vv{\omega}} = \Delta\hat{\vv{\theta}}_k/\Delta t$ and
$\hat{\vv{f}} = \Delta\hat{\vv{v}}_k/\Delta t$ the cycle averages,
\begin{equation}
  \mat{F} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    -\skewm{\hat{\vv{\omega}}} & \mat{0} & \mat{0} & -\mat{I}_3 &
      \mat{0} \\
    -\hat{\mat{C}}\skewm{\hat{\vv{f}}} & \mat{0} &
      \mat{G}(\hat{\vv{p}}) & \mat{0} & -\hat{\mat{C}} \\
    \mat{0} & \mat{I}_3 & \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{0} \\
    \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{0} & -\mat{I}_3/\tau_{c,g} & \mat{0} \\
    \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{0} & -\mat{I}_3/\tau_{c,a}
  \end{bmatrix},
  \label{eq:ekf:F}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \mat{\Gamma} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    -\mat{I}_3 & \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{0} \\
    \mat{0} & -\hat{\mat{C}} & \mat{0} & \mat{0} \\
    \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{0} \\
    \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{I}_3 & \mat{0} \\
    \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{I}_3
  \end{bmatrix},
  \qquad
  \mat{Q}_c = \mathrm{diag}\bigl(
    N_g^2\,\mat{I}_3,\;
    N_a^2\,\mat{I}_3,\;
    q_g\,\mat{I}_3,\;
    q_a\,\mat{I}_3\bigr),
  \label{eq:ekf:G}
\end{equation}
where $N_g, N_a$ are the configured ARW/VRW coefficients
(equation~\eqref{eq:imu:arw}) and $q = 2\sigma_{\mathrm{GM}}^2/\tau_c$
is the continuous-time Gauss--Markov drive density consistent with the
discrete recursion~\eqref{eq:imu:gm} (Maybeck~\cite{maybeck1979}).

\subsection{Discrete propagation and updates}
\label{sec:ekf:updates}

Discretization, first order by decision (assumption 5):
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \mat{\Phi}_k = \mat{I}_{15} + \mat{F}\,\Delta t,
  \qquad
  \mat{Q}_k = \mat{\Gamma}\,\mat{Q}_c\,\mat{\Gamma}^{\mathsf T}\,
    \Delta t,
  \qquad
  \mat{P}_k^{-} = \mat{\Phi}_k \mat{P}_{k-1} \mat{\Phi}_k^{\mathsf T}
    + \mat{Q}_k,
  \;}
  \label{eq:ekf:disc}
\end{equation}
with $\mat{P} \leftarrow \tfrac12(\mat{P} + \mat{P}^{\mathsf T})$
after every propagation and update (symmetry pinning against rounding
drift). Updates process the valid measurements of each cycle
sequentially in the fixed order \emph{nav fix, star tracker,
altimeter}, each against the running post-update covariance; invalid
samples (Chapter~\ref{ch:sensors-optical} flags) are skipped. Every
update uses the Joseph-form covariance update, chosen over the short
form for guaranteed symmetric positive semi-definite results:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \vv{y} = \vv{z} - \vv{h}(\hat{\vv{x}}),
  \quad
  \mat{S} = \mat{H}\mat{P}^{-}\mat{H}^{\mathsf T} + \mat{R},
  \quad
  \mat{K} = \mat{P}^{-}\mat{H}^{\mathsf T}\mat{S}^{-1},
  \quad
  \delta\hat{\vv{x}} = \mat{K}\,\vv{y},
  \quad
  \mat{P}^{+} = (\mat{I} - \mat{K}\mat{H})\,\mat{P}^{-}\,
    (\mat{I} - \mat{K}\mat{H})^{\mathsf T}
    + \mat{K}\mat{R}\mat{K}^{\mathsf T}.
  \;}
  \label{eq:ekf:joseph}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{External nav fix} ($m = 6$): $\vv{z} =
[\vv{r}_{\mathrm{meas}}; \vv{v}_{\mathrm{meas}}]$, $\vv{h} =
[\hat{\vv{p}}; \hat{\vv{v}}]$, and over the
ordering~\eqref{eq:ekf:staterr}
\begin{equation}
  \mat{H}_{\mathrm{fix}} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{I}_3 & \mat{0} & \mat{0} \\
    \mat{0} & \mat{I}_3 & \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{0}
  \end{bmatrix},
  \qquad
  \mat{R}_{\mathrm{fix}} = \mathrm{diag}\bigl(\sigma_{r,x}^2,
    \sigma_{r,y}^2, \sigma_{r,z}^2,
    \sigma_{v,x}^2, \sigma_{v,y}^2, \sigma_{v,z}^2\bigr),
  \label{eq:ekf:navfixH}
\end{equation}
position rows first. The optional Gauss--Markov fix component
(Chapter~\ref{ch:sensors-radio}) is not modeled by the reference filter
and must be disabled in consistency-gate scenarios.

\paragraph{Star tracker} ($m = 3$): the measurement model is
equation~\eqref{eq:optical:stmodel}. The filter predicts the aberrated
attitude with its own state, $\quat{q}_{\mathrm{pred}} =
\quat{q}_{\mathrm{ab}}(\hat{\vv{\beta}}, \hat{\vv{b}}\mathrm{ore})
\quatmul \hat{\quat{q}}$ --- $\quat{q}_{\mathrm{ab}}$ per
equation~\eqref{eq:optical:qab} with $\hat{\vv{\beta}}$ assembled from
$\hat{\vv{v}}$ and the same ephemeris chain
(equation~\eqref{eq:optical:beta}); the velocity-estimate contribution
to the aberration error is $\norm{\delta\vv{v}}/c \approx
\qty{3.3e-9}{\radian}$ per \unit{\metre\per\second}, negligible ---
and forms the innovation by exact error extraction:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \delta\quat{q}_y = \quat{q}_{\mathrm{pred}}^{-1} \quatmul
    \quat{q}_{\mathrm{meas}},
  \qquad
  \vv{y}_{\mathrm{st}} = 2\,\operatorname{sgn}(\delta q_{y,w})\,
    \delta\vv{q}_{y,v},
  \;}
  \label{eq:ekf:stinnov}
\end{equation}
with $\operatorname{sgn}(0) = +1$. Substituting the models:
$\delta\quat{q}_y = \hat{\quat{q}}^{-1} \quatmul \quat{q}_{\mathrm{true}}
\quatmul \delta\quat{q}_n = \delta\quat{q}(\delta\vv{\theta}) \quatmul
\delta\quat{q}_n$, so $\vv{y}_{\mathrm{st}} = \delta\vv{\theta} +
\vv{\epsilon}_n + O(\norm{\cdot}^2)$ and
\begin{equation}
  \mat{H}_{\mathrm{st}} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    \mat{I}_3 & \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \mat{0}
  \end{bmatrix},
  \qquad
  \mat{R}_{\mathrm{st}} = \mathrm{diag}\bigl(\sigma_1^2, \sigma_2^2,
    \sigma_3^2\bigr).
  \label{eq:ekf:stH}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Altimeter} ($m = 1$): $z = h_{\mathrm{meas}}$, $h(\hat{\vv{x}})
= h_{\mathrm{geo}}(\hat{\vv{p}})$, the same Bowring conversion as the
sensor (equation~\eqref{eq:radio:alt}). The gradient of geodetic height
with respect to Cartesian position is exactly the unit ellipsoidal
normal at the sub-vehicle point, so
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \mat{H}_{\mathrm{alt}} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    \mat{0} & \mat{0} & \hat{\vv{n}}^{I\,\mathsf T} & \mat{0} & \mat{0}
  \end{bmatrix},
  \;}
  \qquad
  \hat{\vv{n}}^{I} = {\dcm{I}{E}}^{\mathsf T}
  \begin{bmatrix}
    \cos\hat\phi\cos\hat\lambda \\
    \cos\hat\phi\sin\hat\lambda \\
    \sin\hat\phi
  \end{bmatrix},
  \qquad
  R_{\mathrm{alt}} = \sigma_h^2 ,
  \label{eq:ekf:altH}
\end{equation}
with $(\hat\phi, \hat\lambda)$ the geodetic latitude and longitude of
$\hat{\vv{p}}$ from the same conversion. The altimeter turn-on bias is
not estimated; when enabled, $\sigma_b^2$ is added to $R_{\mathrm{alt}}$
as inflation and the residual mismatch is accepted (gate scenarios
disable it, assumption 3).

\paragraph{Closed-loop reset.} After each update the correction is
folded into the nominal state and the error mean returns to zero:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \hat{\quat{q}} \leftarrow \frac{\hat{\quat{q}} \quatmul
    \delta\quat{q}(\delta\hat{\vv{\theta}})}{\norm{\cdot}},
  \quad
  \hat{\vv{v}} \leftarrow \hat{\vv{v}} + \delta\hat{\vv{v}},
  \quad
  \hat{\vv{p}} \leftarrow \hat{\vv{p}} + \delta\hat{\vv{p}},
  \quad
  \hat{\vv{b}} \leftarrow \hat{\vv{b}} + \delta\hat{\vv{b}},
  \quad
  \delta\hat{\vv{x}} \leftarrow \vv{0},
  \;}
  \label{eq:ekf:reset}
\end{equation}
using the same exact map~\eqref{eq:gnc:exactrot}. The exact reset also
transports the covariance by a Jacobian that differs from identity only
in the attitude block, by $O(\delta\hat{\vv{\theta}})$
(Sol\`a~\cite{sola2017}); the reference filter omits this correction,
a second-order effect in the post-update error, and the omission is
part of the specification.

\subsection{Consistency statistics: NEES, NIS, and the chi-square
gates}
\label{sec:ekf:consistency}

Per FR-26 the filter logs, every cycle, the total-state estimate
\texttt{nav.est.x\_hat} $= [\hat{\quat{q}}\ (4,\ \text{scalar-first}),\
\hat{\vv{v}}\ (3),\ \hat{\vv{p}}\ (3),\ \hat{\vv{b}}_g\ (3),\
\hat{\vv{b}}_a\ (3)]$ (16 values) and the error covariance
\texttt{nav.est.P} as the row-major packed upper triangle of the
$15 \times 15$ matrix (120 values), and, per update, the innovation
pair \texttt{nav.innov.y}, \texttt{nav.innov.S} (packed upper triangle)
tagged by sensor --- all inside the FR-16 reserved groups, by these
names.

\texttt{star consistency} reconstructs the true estimation error from
the \texttt{truth} channels and the logged sensor-bias diagnostics
(Chapter~\ref{ch:sensors-imu}), using the same error conventions:
$\delta\vv{\theta}_k$ by the extraction~\eqref{eq:optical:extract}
applied to $\hat{\quat{q}}_k^{-1} \quatmul \quat{q}_{\mathrm{true},k}$,
additive differences elsewhere. The statistics
(Bar-Shalom, Li, and Kirubarajan~\cite{barshalom2001}) are the
normalized estimation error squared and the normalized innovation
squared,
\begin{equation}
  \epsilon_k = \delta\vv{x}_k^{\mathsf T}\, \mat{P}_k^{-1}\,
    \delta\vv{x}_k
  \quad (n = 15),
  \label{eq:ekf:nees}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \nu_k = \vv{y}_k^{\mathsf T}\, \mat{S}_k^{-1}\, \vv{y}_k
  \quad (n = m_k, \text{ the update dimension}),
  \label{eq:ekf:nis}
\end{equation}
each distributed $\chi^2_n$ at every epoch for a consistent filter.
Two aggregations are computed:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Per-run time average} $\bar\epsilon = \tfrac1K
    \sum_k \epsilon_k$ (and likewise per sensor for $\nu$): reported as
    a \emph{diagnostic} with indicative bounds, because successive
    errors of one run are serially correlated and the independence
    assumption behind chi-square bounds does not hold across time
    within a run~\cite{barshalom2001}.
  \item \textbf{$N$-run ensemble average} at each epoch (and averaged
    over epochs for the headline number): for $N$ independent seeded
    runs, $N\bar\epsilon_N = \sum_{i=1}^{N}\epsilon^{(i)} \sim
    \chi^2_{Nn}$ exactly under consistency, giving the two-sided
    95\,\% acceptance gate
    \begin{equation}
      \boxed{\;
      \frac{\chi^2_{0.025}(Nn)}{N}
        \;\le\; \bar\epsilon_N \;\le\;
      \frac{\chi^2_{0.975}(Nn)}{N},
      \;}
      \label{eq:ekf:ensemble}
    \end{equation}
    the acceptance form for both NEES ($n = 15$) and NIS ($n = m$ per
    sensor). For the exit-criterion-3 ensemble, $N = 100$: NEES bounds
    $[13.95,\; 16.09]$; NIS bounds $[5.34,\; 6.70]$ for the nav fix
    ($n = 6$), $[2.54,\; 3.50]$ for the star tracker ($n = 3$), and
    $[0.74,\; 1.30]$ for the altimeter ($n = 1$).
\end{itemize}
Chi-square quantiles are evaluated without external dependencies
(D-12) by the Wilson--Hilferty transformation~\cite{wilson1931},
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \chi^2_p(k) \approx k \left( 1 - \frac{2}{9k}
    + z_p \sqrt{\frac{2}{9k}} \right)^{\!3},
  \;}
  \qquad
  z_{0.975} = -z_{0.025} = 1.959963984540054,
  \label{eq:ekf:wh}
\end{equation}
whose relative error against the exact quantiles is below
$5 \times 10^{-5}$ for $k \ge 300$ (verified numerically during
drafting) --- four orders below the gate widths. The ensemble driver
initializes each run's estimate by perturbing the known true initial
state with a seeded draw from $\mathcal{N}(\vv{0}, \mat{P}_0)$
(attitude via equation~\eqref{eq:ekf:qerr}), so the initial error is
distributed exactly as the filter believes --- the precondition for
NEES consistency from the first cycle.

\section{Discretization and implementation}
\label{sec:ekf:implementation}

Binding implementation notes for the Phase~6 module:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Module and traceability.} The filter is a built-in
    C++ \texttt{IGncComponent} (navigation role) in the FR-25
    registry; \texttt{star consistency} is Python-side, reading only
    logs. Source comments echo \texttt{eq:ekf:qerr},
    \texttt{eq:ekf:mech}, \texttt{eq:ekf:errdyn}, \texttt{eq:ekf:F},
    \texttt{eq:ekf:disc}, \texttt{eq:ekf:joseph},
    \texttt{eq:ekf:stinnov}, \texttt{eq:ekf:altH},
    \texttt{eq:ekf:reset}, and the consistency evaluator echoes
    \texttt{eq:ekf:nees}, \texttt{eq:ekf:nis},
    \texttt{eq:ekf:ensemble}, \texttt{eq:ekf:wh}.
  \item \textbf{Cycle order, normative.} (1) mechanize the nominal
    state, equation~\eqref{eq:ekf:mech}; (2) propagate the covariance,
    equation~\eqref{eq:ekf:disc}, with $\mat{F}$ evaluated at the
    pre-cycle nominal and the cycle-average $\hat{\vv{\omega}}$,
    $\hat{\vv{f}}$; (3) process valid updates in the fixed order nav
    fix, star tracker, altimeter, each as
    equations~\eqref{eq:ekf:joseph}--\eqref{eq:ekf:reset} with an
    immediate reset; (4) log estimate, covariance, and innovations.
  \item \textbf{Linear algebra.} Fixed-size Eigen operations only; the
    $\mat{S}$ solves use \texttt{Eigen::LDLT} on the (at most
    $6 \times 6$) innovation covariance --- deterministic, no pivoting
    hazards at these sizes; no heap allocation in the cycle path
    (D-10). Every Eigen product is bound to a named concrete type,
    never \texttt{auto} (the Phase~4 dangling-expression lesson).
  \item \textbf{No truth access.} The filter sees only
    \texttt{GncInput}; the consistency evaluator, not the filter,
    consumes \texttt{truth} channels offline. The \texttt{oracle}
    flag exists for debugging other components and is never read by
    the reference filter.
  \item \textbf{Determinism.} Same seed, same binary: identical
    \texttt{nav.*} channels byte-for-byte; the exit-criterion-3
    ensemble rerun is bit-identical because every run's draws come
    from named streams keyed by the manifest seed schedule.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Validation evidence}
\label{sec:ekf:validation}

This chapter precedes its module; the validation-evidence table with
machine-checked test identifiers is added by the implementing change.
The planned gates, mirroring Phase~6 exit criterion~3 (with criteria
5 and~6 adjacent):
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Ensemble NEES/NIS (exit criterion 3).} On the reference
    consistency scenario (point-mass truth environment, turn-on biases
    off, all three aiding sensors active), a committed pytest driver
    executes a 100-run seeded ensemble and gates
    equation~\eqref{eq:ekf:ensemble}: NEES within $[13.95, 16.09]$ and
    per-sensor NIS within their stated bounds; rerunning the driver
    reproduces every statistic bit-for-bit.
  \item \textbf{Mechanization against dead reckoning.} With aiding
    disabled and zero sensor errors, the filter's attitude equals the
    Chapter~\ref{ch:gnc-builtin} dead reckoner exactly (shared code
    path), and velocity/position follow the truth propagator within
    the documented mechanization residuals on a short arc.
  \item \textbf{Update algebra.} Single-update unit tests against an
    independent NumPy implementation of
    equations~\eqref{eq:ekf:joseph}--\eqref{eq:ekf:reset} for each
    sensor at committed states: gain, posterior covariance
    (Joseph form), and reset all match to tight tolerances; the
    star-tracker innovation of equation~\eqref{eq:ekf:stinnov}
    reproduces an injected $\delta\vv{\theta}$ exactly in the
    noise-free case, including the aberration prediction.
  \item \textbf{Covariance hygiene.} Long-run propagation preserves
    symmetry to rounding and positive semi-definiteness (smallest
    eigenvalue bounded) under alternating updates and outages.
  \item \textbf{Quantile evaluator.} The Wilson--Hilferty values of
    equation~\eqref{eq:ekf:wh} match tabulated exact quantiles to the
    stated $5\times10^{-5}$ relative bound across the used $(p, k)$
    set.
  \item \textbf{Innovation logging (FR-26).} Logged \texttt{nav.innov}
    channels round-trip through the loader and reproduce the
    filter-internal values exactly; \texttt{star consistency} on a
    single run reports the per-run diagnostics and exits nonzero when
    fed an inconsistent (deliberately mistuned) run --- the gate is
    shown able to fail.
\end{itemize}

\section{References}
\label{sec:ekf:references}

The error-state (multiplicative) EKF formulation follows
Sol\`a~\cite{sola2017} and the MEKF heritage in Markley and
Crassidis~\cite{markley2014}, transcribed to the D-7 conventions of
Chapter~\ref{ch:notation}; strapdown mechanization terms follow
Titterton and Weston~\cite{titterton2004} and
Savage~\cite{savage1998b}; Gauss--Markov process algebra follows
Maybeck~\cite{maybeck1979}; NEES/NIS consistency testing and its
chi-square acceptance bounds follow Bar-Shalom, Li, and
Kirubarajan~\cite{barshalom2001}; the quantile approximation is
Wilson and Hilferty~\cite{wilson1931}; the rejected higher-order
discretization is Van Loan~\cite{vanloan1978}. Full bibliographic
details appear in the bibliography.
