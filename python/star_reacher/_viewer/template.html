star_reacher playback viewer

Plays back one SRLOG run. Playback consumes only the log — nothing is re-simulated. This file is self-contained and makes zero network requests.

Camera modes

  • 1 free orbit — drag to orbit the central body, wheel to zoom.
  • 2 chase — follows the spacecraft from behind its velocity vector; wheel sets the follow distance.
  • 3 body-fixed — camera rides the spacecraft body frame (rotates with the logged attitude), looking along body +x; wheel sets the offset distance.
  • 4 nadir — from above the spacecraft on the body-center line, looking down at the central body.

Keys

  • space play/pause; ←/→ step ±0.5 % of the run.
  • a axes triads, v velocity vector, f thrust/per-source force vectors, t trail, g groundtrack, h this help.

Overlays

  • axes — spacecraft body triad (x red, y green, z blue) from the logged attitude quaternion, plus the inertial triad at the central body center.
  • velocity — inertial velocity direction at the spacecraft.
  • forces — per-source body-frame forces from the log's forces group, rotated to the inertial scene by the logged attitude. Arrow lengths are log-scaled for visibility (magnitudes in the legend are exact); absent when the log carries no forces group.
  • trail — flown keyframe path up to the current time.
  • groundtrack — sub-spacecraft track on the central-body sphere.

Display-grade approximations

  • Playback interpolation between keyframes — linear position, spherical-linear (slerp) attitude — is display-only and NON-PHYSICAL. Analysis must read the log, never this viewer.
  • The view stream is decimated: .
  • Earth rotation for the groundtrack and coastline uses the Earth Rotation Angle only (UT1≈UTC; no precession-nutation, no polar motion) — display-grade, about a degree of longitude at the 2020s epochs. Bodies without a rotation model show the inertial sub-point.
  • HUD altitude is geocentric (above the mean equatorial radius), not geodetic. Lighting is fixed, not the run's sun geometry.
